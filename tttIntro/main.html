(The What) What is Trouble in Terrorist Town?

Before I can teach you how to play, I must teach you how the game works.

Role Goal Advantage
Innocent Survive and identify Traitors. Power in numbers.
Traitor Eliminate all Innocents. Access to the "C" shop and knowing where your allies are.
Detective Find the Traitors using forensic tools. They are the only proven Innocent. Also gets a shop

"When the game starts, a small number of players is selected as Traitors, who have to kill all the Innocent players (ie. the rest of the players). The Innocent players know they are in the majority, but they do not know who is a Traitor and who is not."

(The Why) Why Should I Care? I'm Already Having Fun

Personally, I dislike shooters like CSGO or Rainbow Six Siege. Many times I will die 15-30 seconds into the round. Not only is it frustrating, it can be boring. I start to wonder why I'm even playing this game. It feels like I'm spending more time watching my friends or randoms play than actually playing the game. While making this website, I realized the same strategies I must admit that games like these can be just as fun as TTT when implementing proper tactics. Imagine you're teammates consistently including you in plays, keeping you engaged, and giving you jobs that feel important. I bet you're going to enjoy the game a lot more.

Okay, maybe you have no idea what I'm talking about and shooters are just straight fun for you. In that case, you're probably the one making the craziest plays already and don't need that someone to include you. This isn't viable for a fun session for everyone in a gaming group though. If we play TTT like a straight shooter, this is the most likely outcome. The top dogs like Alex or Ben are going to dominate the lobby, and those without the resources are left to sit out the rounds and watch events unfold with no feeling of having contributed or being a part of the story.

Now, I'm not going to say to you that you have to accommodate these newbies or change your entire playstyle just because I think it's wrong. Having extensively thought about this, I actually don't dislike your strategies. I think almost every strategy is viable in TTT, even the "I pick up gun and shoot" strategy. What I believe is important that even top vets like you need to know is the purpose of each of these strategies in the universe of TTT. The fact of the matter is the top dogs in this friend group thankfully don't just dominate the lobby. They change up their tactics, they try new things. Sometimes they just play chill and let things unfold after having played a crazy round. My goal is that you listening to what I have to say about different strategies will enlighten you an even more intricate schemes you can devise.

We all know that if you top players were to play straight killer style every time, i.e. the moment you catch somebody alone you eliminate them, it eventually would get old and no longer present any challenge. Thankfully, this game is naturally fun and naturally has funny moments. I want to push that even further, but not in a way that makes it artifical or daunting to any of you since that would achieve the opposite of what I want. I want every person in the lobby to have access to the resources, ideas, and understanding that will give them what they want (enjoying the game) and give me what I want. Well, whether you're a seasoned operative or a newcomer to the world of espionage, this guide will teach you the skills needed to survive and thrive in the dangerous realm of betrayal.
